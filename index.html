A LITTLE BIT ABOUT ME


I'm a first year student at Stony Brook University in Stony Brook, New York, studying Computer Engineering with a Minor in History.

Currently, I am the Software Team Lead for the Stony Brook Robot Design Team where I am helping to build a Mars Rover for the University Rover Challenge 2019. Additionally, I am the Social Media and Technology Intern for the Center for Civic Justice at Stony Brook University, where I lead the center's marketing strategy and social media content development.

My journey through life began in the waning years of the 20th century, the year 1999 to be specific. I was born and raised in the port city of Chittagong in the vibrant country of Bangladesh.

In 2008, I had the awesome chance to come and live in the city that never sleeps, NYC, and that's where I have been ever since.

Moreover, I am a graduate of Stuyvesant High School (Class of 2017) and a CollegePoint Scholar.

In my work, I believe that the key to success is the coming together of every team member to accomplish the common goal of creating the best product possible.

And, in achieving that goal, one must achieve a strong understanding of the infrastructure and the hardware that the product will be running on. Only then can one truly create pixel-perfect software that provides great experiences to every user.

When I am not in front of a screen, I am probably taking a walk on NYC's busy streets, eating ice-cream or poring over a history book.
